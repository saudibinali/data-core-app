SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE TECHNICAL SUMMARY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Phase 11 built a complete, six-layer enterprise governance and compliance
architecture on top of the operational resilience infrastructure from Phase 10.
The architecture converts raw platform events into a forensically defensible,
audit-ready governance record — without any autonomous enforcement, external
integrations, AI, or legal automation.

The six sub-phases are layered and interdependent. Each sub-phase adds one
distinct governance capability on top of the one below it:

  P11-A  Record        — Every significant platform event is captured in an
                         immutable, SHA-256 hash-linked audit chain. Tampering
                         with any stored record is immediately detectable.

  P11-B  Detect        — The accumulated audit records are evaluated against
                         eight built-in governance policies. Violations are
                         classified by severity and linked to specific evidence.

  P11-C  Orchestrate   — Each detected violation can have a human-governed
                         workflow opened against it. Operators acknowledge,
                         investigate, escalate, and resolve violations through
                         a six-state lifecycle. Every transition requires explicit
                         operator attribution.

  P11-D  Observe       — The aggregate of all workflow data is continuously
                         analyzed to produce governance health metrics: escalation
                         rates, resolution throughput, policy breach frequency,
                         workflow effectiveness scores, and per-policy stability
                         ratings. All read-only.

  P11-E  Consolidate   — All four layers are mapped into a unified topology
                         profile. Each layer's declared boundary invariants are
                         verified in isolation. A cross-layer enforcement check
                         confirms that no layer in the stack declares autonomous
                         enforcement. A readiness profile classifies the platform's
                         overall governance posture as not_ready / partial / ready /
                         production_ready.

  P11-F  Package       — The entire governance record is assembled into structured,
                         integrity-hashed evidence packages suitable for auditor
                         review. Six evidence scopes control which sections are
                         included. Each package carries a SHA-256 content hash.
                         Topology snapshots can be captured and compared using a
                         point-in-time diff engine.

At closure, the platform possesses a governance foundation capable of:
  - Detecting and classifying policy violations in real time
  - Tracking the full lifecycle of every violation investigation
  - Measuring governance health across 13 operational metrics
  - Verifying that every governance layer upholds its declared boundary
  - Producing structured, hashable evidence packages for review or export

No automated enforcement. No legal conclusions. No external submission.
The stack is explicitly designed to support human decision-making, not replace it.

2.1  P11-A — Immutable Audit Chain & Forensic Integrity
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Objective:
  Convert scattered observability events (P10-A through P10-F) into a forensic
  governance record that is tamper-detectable and suitable for compliance audit.

What P11-A provides:
  - ImmutableAuditChain — hash-linked entries stored in compliance_audit_chains
  - Each entry: currentAuditHash = SHA-256(prevHash | eventType | entityId |
    operatorId | occurredAt | JSON.stringify(payload, sortedKeys))
  - If any stored field is modified after INSERT, the recomputed hash will not
    match the stored hash — tamper is immediately detectable
  - GENESIS entries (first in a chain) use "GENESIS" as the prevHash input
  - AuditIntegrityStatus — 5 tiers: verified / warning / compromised /
    orphaned / incomplete
  - verifyAuditIntegrity() — recomputes all hashes, detects orphans, returns
    a full AuditIntegrityReport with per-entry results and overallStatus
  - detectTamperAnomalies() — targeted hash-comparison that returns only the
    entries with divergent hashes (severity: "compromised")
  - detectOrphanedRecords() — finds entries whose previousAuditHash references
    a hash not present in the evaluated entry set
  - RetentionClassification — 4-tier keyword-based classification:
    operational / governance / compliance_sensitive / forensic_critical
  - classifyRetention(entityType, eventType) — deterministic, pure, reusable by P11-B
  - reconstructAuditTimeline(entityId, entries) — chronological forensic replay,
    sorted by occurredAt (not recordedAt), fully annotated with per-event
    integrityStatus and retentionClassification
  - ComplianceSummary — aggregate counts by integrityStatus and retentionClassification

  artifacts/api-server/src/lib/workflows/compliance-audit-integrity.ts

  lib/db/src/schema/compliance-audit-chains.ts

  APPEND-ONLY: no UPDATE or DELETE ever issued against this table

2.2  P11-B — Policy Governance & Violation Intelligence
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Objective:
  Add a policy evaluation layer that determines which governance policies are
  violated by the current state of audit chains and execution records.

What P11-B provides:
  - GovernancePolicy — 8 built-in policies (POL-001 through POL-008) defined
    as immutable in-code constants (not stored in DB)
  - PolicyCategory — 5 domains: audit_integrity, execution_governance,
    retention_compliance, forensic_coverage, operational_continuity
  - PolicySeverity — critical / high / medium / low
  - PolicyScope — workspace / platform / entity
  - evaluateGovernancePolicies(input, now) → GovernanceViolation[] — runs all
    8 detectors and returns violations sorted critical-first

  The 8 detectors and the policies they enforce:
    POL-001 (critical)  detectMissingAuditChains()
                        → execution attempt has no corresponding audit entry
    POL-002 (high)      detectOrphanedIntegrityChains()
                        → audit entry with previousAuditHash referencing nothing
    POL-003 (critical)  detectRollbackWithoutConfirmation()
                        → rolled_back execution with no confirmedBy
    POL-004 (medium)    detectRetentionMisclassification()
                        → stored retention ≠ recomputed classifyRetention()
    POL-005 (critical)  detectTamperedIntegrityChains()
                        → audit entry with integrityStatus="compromised"
    POL-006 (high)      detectForensicCoverageGaps()
                        → rollback with no forensic_critical audit entry
    POL-007 (high)      detectUnresolvedCriticalIncidents()
                        → forensic_critical audit entry with non-verified status
    POL-008 (medium)    detectExecutionOrchestrationGaps()
                        → execution attempt with no actionId (not linked to orchestration)

  - GovernanceViolation — violationId, policyId, workspaceId, entityId,
    violationType, severity, detectedAt, evidenceReferences[], violationStatus
  - GovernanceSummary — aggregate counts by severity + violationType, overallRiskLevel
  - resolveEvidenceReferences(violation, auditEntries, executionRecords)
    → { auditEvidence, executionEvidence, unresolved } — links violations to
    their source records for full evidence context
  - workspaceIdFilter — violations can be scoped to a single workspace

2.3  P11-C — Human-Governed Compliance Workflow Orchestration
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Objective:
  Give operators a persistent, fully attributed lifecycle for each violation
  detected by P11-B — from opening an investigation through escalation to final
  resolution or dismissal.

What P11-C provides:
  - GovernanceWorkflowAction — full workflow model with 6-state lifecycle and
    complete attribution chain (initiatedBy, acknowledgedBy/At, escalatedBy/At,
    resolvedBy/At, resolutionClassification, resolutionNote)
  - GovernanceWorkflowStatus — 6 states:
    open → acknowledged → under_review → escalated → resolved | dismissed
  - GovernanceEscalationLevel — 4 tiers:
    informational < standard < elevated < critical (monotonic — can only increase)
  - ResolutionClassification — 5 values:
    confirmed_violation / false_positive / operational_exception /
    policy_gap / unresolved_pending_review
  - State machine functions (all pure — return new value objects, never mutate input):
    initiateGovernanceWorkflow()  — open state, one row per violationId
    acknowledgeWorkflow()         — open/acknowledged → acknowledged/under_review
    escalateWorkflow()            — any non-terminal → escalated
    resolveWorkflow()             — any non-terminal → resolved or dismissed
  - All transitions require a non-empty operatorId — EMPTY_OPERATOR error otherwise
  - Fail-closed: any invalid transition returns { updated: null, errors: [...] }
  - Terminal state finality: resolved/dismissed block all further transitions (409)
  - buildWorkflowSummary() — aggregate counts including criticalUnresolved metric

2.5  P11-E — Governance Topology, Boundary Verification & Readiness
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Objective:
  Consolidate all four prior layers into a unified topology profile, verify
  each layer's declared boundary invariants, run a cross-layer enforcement
  check, and produce a single readiness verdict for the entire governance stack.

What P11-E provides:
  - GOVERNANCE_LAYERS — static registry of 4 GovernanceLayerDescriptor entries:
    P11-A (integrity_layer / record), P11-B (policy_layer / detect),
    P11-C (workflow_layer / orchestrate), P11-D (analytics_layer / observe)
  - GovernanceBoundaryProperty — 7 verifiable invariants:
    append_only, read_only, human_governed, deterministic, fail_closed,
    no_enforcement, no_ai
  - verifyLayerBoundary(layer, expectedProperties, warnings) — fail-closed:
    if any expected property is absent → boundary_leak_detected (not warning)
    GovernanceBoundaryStatus: verified / warning / boundary_leak_detected / incomplete
  - Cross-layer enforcement check — a synthetic CROSS descriptor is built from
    properties universally declared by ALL 4 layers. verifyLayerBoundary(CROSS,
    ["no_enforcement", "no_ai"]) must return "verified" at all times.
  - GovernanceLifecycleCoverage — runtime coverage evidence:
    auditRecordsTotal, workflowsTotal, activeWorkflows, resolvedWorkflows,
    criticalUnresolved, activeLayers (2–4), coverageScore (4-tier)
  - GovernanceCoverageScore — partial / substantial / comprehensive
  - GovernanceTopologyProfile — full topology snapshot:
    governanceLayers[], integrityBoundaries, policyBoundaries,
    workflowBoundaries, analyticsBoundaries, enforcementBoundaries,
    observabilityCoverage, lifecycleCoverage
  - ObservabilityCoverage — static map of 16 event types across P11-A through P11-D
    (P11-E counts 4 more as its own; P11-F adds 4 more — total 24 by P11-F closure)
  - GovernanceBoundarySummary — aggregated across all 5 checks (4 layers + cross):
    totalLayers, verifiedLayers, warningLayers, leakDetectedLayers,
    incompleteLayers, overallStatus, byLayer[]
  - GovernanceReadinessProfile — final readiness verdict:
    not_ready / partial / ready / production_ready
    Fail-closed: any boundary_leak_detected → not_ready
    production_ready requires: all verified + substantial/comprehensive coverage
    + observabilityComplete
  - buildGovernanceTopology(), buildBoundarySummary(), buildGovernanceReadiness()
    — three pure functions composable in sequence, all no-I/O

2.6  P11-F — Governance Evidence Packaging & Audit Export Readiness

Objective:

What P11-F provides:
  - GovernanceEvidencePackageScope — 6 scopes:
    platform (7 sections) / workspace (5) / entity (3) / violation (2) /
    workflow (1) / readiness (2)
  - GovernanceEvidenceSection — 7 section types (one per governance concern):
    audit_integrity, policy_violations, workflow_lifecycle, governance_analytics,
    topology_readiness, forensic_timeline, boundary_summary
  - sectionsForScope(scope) — deterministic static map, returns a copy
  - GovernanceEvidencePackage — the full audit bundle:
    packageId, packageScope, workspaceId, entityId, includedSections,
    per-section summaries (audit / violation / workflow / analytics / topology),
    boundaryDetails, evidenceReferences[], warnings[], integrityStatus,
    packageIntegrityHash (SHA-256, 64 chars), generatedBy, generatedAt
  - GovernanceEvidenceReference — cross-layer record pointer with sourceLayer,
    entityType, entityId, description. Up to 7 references per package.
  - buildEvidenceReferences() — condition-gated: a reference is only generated
    if its section is in scope AND the corresponding data is present
  - GovernanceScopedWarning — structured warning with machine-readable code,
    human-readable message, optional layerId, optional section
  - GovernanceWarningCode — 8 codes:
    P11A_NO_RUNTIME_DATA, P11C_NO_RUNTIME_DATA, GOVERNANCE_BOUNDARY_LEAK,
    GOVERNANCE_READINESS_PARTIAL, GOVERNANCE_CRITICAL_UNRESOLVED,
    GOVERNANCE_OBSERVABILITY_INCOMPLETE, EVIDENCE_REFERENCE_MISSING,
    EVIDENCE_SECTION_INCOMPLETE
  - gatherWarnings() — 9 warning conditions evaluated deterministically
  - GovernancePackageIntegrityStatus — 4-tier fail-closed:
    verified / warning / incomplete / compromised
    compromised takes absolute precedence (GOVERNANCE_BOUNDARY_LEAK)
  - classifyPackageIntegrity() — first match wins in strict severity order
  - computePackageIntegrityHash(content) — SHA-256 over sorted-key JSON,
    excludes packageIntegrityHash and generatedAt fields from the hash input
  - buildGovernanceEvidencePackage() — 11-step pure build pipeline, no I/O
  - GovernanceTopologySnapshotPayload — point-in-time topology snapshot with

  - GovernanceTopologyDiff — 7 change dimensions:
    boundaryStatusChanges[], coverageScoreChange, readinessStatusChange,

    criticalGapChanges (added/removed), hasChanges
  - diffGovernanceTopologySnapshots(prev, next, now) — pure, no mutation,
    deterministic ordering (all added/removed arrays sorted alphabetically)

3.1  How Layers Depend on Each Other

  Each layer reads ONLY from the layer(s) below it, and only in one direction:

  P11-A  reads from: P10 platform events (entityType/eventType supplied by caller)
  P11-B  reads from: P11-A (compliance_audit_chains) + P10-E execution records
  P11-C  reads from: P11-B violation output (violationId/policyId supplied by caller)
  P11-D  reads from: P11-C (governance_workflow_actions) + P11-B policy constants
  P11-E  reads from: P11-A + P11-C (count(*) only) + GOVERNANCE_LAYERS static registry
  P11-F  reads from: P11-A + P11-C (counts + aggregates) + P11-E topology output

  No layer inverts this dependency. No layer writes to a layer above it.
  No layer can trigger a state change in any other layer.

3.2  How Boundaries Remain Separated

  Each sub-phase was built with one principle: add capability without crossing
  the boundary of the layer below. Specifically:

  - P11-B never writes to compliance_audit_chains (P11-A's table)
  - P11-C never re-evaluates policies (P11-B's function)
  - P11-D never transitions a workflow (P11-C's function)
  - P11-E never computes analytics (P11-D's function)
  - P11-F never verifies audit integrity (P11-A's function), never runs policy
    evaluation (P11-B's function), never transitions workflows (P11-C's function),
    and never computes analytics (P11-D's function)

  The route handler (platform.ts) is the ONLY place where multiple layer
  functions are composed. This is intentional — the route handler acts as a
  thin orchestration layer that fetches data, calls pure engine functions in
  sequence, and returns the result.

4.7  Super-Admin Boundary Summary
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All 17 governance routes require role = super_admin.
No governance route is accessible by admin, manager, or member roles.
No governance route returns data from any other tenant's workspace without
  an explicit super_admin call specifying a workspaceId.
No governance route writes to any table other than:
  compliance_audit_chains (POST /audit-chains — APPEND-ONLY, INSERT only)
  governance_workflow_actions (POST /workflows — INSERT;
    PATCH /acknowledge /escalate /resolve — UPDATE with attribution fields only)
No governance route calls any external service, webhook, or HTTP endpoint.
No governance route auto-escalates, auto-resolves, or auto-dismisses any workflow.

SECTION 6 — FINAL SAFETY BOUNDARIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These boundaries are non-negotiable. They must be preserved in all future
phases, enhancements, frontend additions, and export layers. Violating any
of these boundaries changes the fundamental character of the governance stack
from advisory to enforcement — which would require a full compliance and legal
review before implementation.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1  APPEND-ONLY (P11-A)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The compliance_audit_chains table is INSERT-ONLY. No UPDATE, no DELETE,
no TRUNCATE has ever been issued against it and none may be issued in future
phases.

Why this matters:
  The audit chain's tamper-detection depends on the guarantee that persisted
  rows are never modified. If an UPDATE is possible, a sufficiently privileged
  attacker could modify both the data and the stored hash, making tamper
  detection ineffective.

Permissible future changes:
  - Adding new columns with DEFAULT values (migration only, does not affect
    existing rows or hash computation)
  - Adding new indexes
  - Querying with new filters

Not permissible:
  - UPDATE of any column in any existing row
  - DELETE of any row (except via workspace CASCADE on workspace deletion —
    which is intentional tenant lifecycle management)
  - Soft-deletion columns that could mask entries from integrity verification

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2  HUMAN-GOVERNED (P11-C)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every state transition in governance_workflow_actions requires an explicit,
non-empty operatorId supplied by a human actor in the request body.

Why this matters:
  The governance workflow exists to provide a documented chain of human
  accountability for every compliance decision. If any transition can be
  performed without a human operatorId, the chain of custody is broken.

Not permissible in any future phase:
  - Auto-acknowledge: any system that automatically transitions a workflow
    from "open" to "acknowledged" without a human operatorId
  - Auto-escalate: any system that calls escalateWorkflow() on a schedule
    or threshold trigger without human initiation
  - Auto-resolve: any system that calls resolveWorkflow() based on a computed
    metric (e.g., "resolved automatically because criticalUnresolved dropped")
  - Auto-dismiss: any system that calls resolveWorkflow(dismiss=true) without
    explicit human operator action

A future phase may add a batch-acknowledge feature — but it must still require
a human operatorId and must be an intentional operator action, not an automatic
system response to a metric threshold.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.3  READ-ONLY (P11-D, P11-E, P11-F)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The analytics, topology, and packaging layers are SELECT-only. They do not
call db.insert(), db.update(), or db.delete() under any code path.

Why this matters:
  The analytics and packaging layers must never have side effects on the
  governance record. An analytics computation that accidentally modifies a
  workflow row would corrupt the very data it is measuring.

Not permissible in any future addition to P11-D, P11-E, or P11-F:
  - DB writes of any kind (including materialized view refresh via INSERT)
  - Caching analytics results in a DB table from within the engine functions
    (caching may be done in the route handler if necessary, but it must use
    a dedicated cache table separate from governance data)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4  NO AI
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All classification, scoring, hashing, and diff computation across P11-A
through P11-F is deterministic, rule-based logic. No machine learning,
probabilistic scoring, language model, or neural network is used.

Why this matters:
  - Determinism: the same inputs must always produce the same outputs.
    Non-deterministic classification (ML models, embeddings) would make it
    impossible to verify or reproduce a past governance decision.
  - Auditability: rule-based logic can be fully explained to an auditor in
    plain language. A neural network classification cannot be explained the
    same way.
  - Legal defensibility: a policy violation detected by a deterministic rule
    is traceable to a specific condition in the data. An ML classification
    carries probabilistic uncertainty that may not be acceptable in a legal or
    compliance context.

This boundary applies to:
  retentionClassification, AuditIntegrityStatus, PolicySeverity,
  GovernanceViolationType, WorkflowEffectivenessScore, PolicyStabilityScore,
  EscalationTrend, GovernanceBoundaryStatus, GovernanceCoverageScore,
  GovernanceReadinessStatus, GovernancePackageIntegrityStatus

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.5  NO AUTO-ENFORCEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

No output from any governance layer (violation, analytics score, readiness
status, evidence package, integrity hash, WARN event) may automatically trigger:
  - A disciplinary action against a user or workspace
  - A suspension, deactivation, or restriction of any account
  - An automatic escalation of a workflow
  - An automatic resolution or dismissal of a violation
  - A system-initiated notification to an external party (regulator, auditor)
  - A change in any operational platform behavior (rate limits, feature flags)

The governance stack is strictly ADVISORY. Its outputs inform human operators.
Human operators decide what to do. The platform does not act on its own
governance findings.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.6  NO REGULATOR INTEGRATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

No governance route, engine function, or event handler submits data to any
external system, regulatory body, or third-party compliance service.

Specifically not implemented and not permissible to add without full legal review:
  - Automatic submission of audit records to a compliance database
  - Automatic reporting of violations to a regulatory authority
  - Integration with SOC 2 or ISO 27001 automated compliance tools
  - Export of evidence packages to a government or legal portal
  - Any HTTP call originating from within P11-A through P11-F engine code

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.7  NO LEGAL CONCLUSIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The governance stack classifies events, detects violations, measures health,
and packages evidence. It does not:
  - Conclude that a law or regulation has been violated
  - Determine legal liability
  - Provide a legal opinion on compliance status
  - Make representations to auditors on behalf of the platform

The evidence packages are structured data. The interpretation of that data
in a legal or regulatory context is a human and legal responsibility, not
an automated platform function.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.8  FAIL-CLOSED
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

In all classification functions across the governance stack, the worst-case
outcome wins when evidence is ambiguous or insufficient:

  P11-A: overallStatus = worst individual status across all evaluated entries
  P11-B: overallRiskLevel = severity of the most severe active violation
  P11-C: any invalid transition returns an error — partial state never written
  P11-D: workflowStabilityScore = worst applicable tier based on thresholds
  P11-E: overallBoundaryStatus = worst across all 5 boundary checks
         GovernanceReadinessStatus = not_ready if any leak, partial if incomplete
  P11-F: integrityStatus = compromised if GOVERNANCE_BOUNDARY_LEAK present
         incomplete if EVIDENCE_SECTION_INCOMPLETE or EVIDENCE_REFERENCE_MISSING
         hasChanges = true if ANY diff dimension shows a change

The fail-closed principle means: when in doubt, report the more severe
classification. It is safer to over-report a warning than to silently
pass an ambiguous state as compliant.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.9  DETERMINISTIC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All pure engine functions in P11-A through P11-F satisfy:

  Same inputs + same `now` parameter → identical JSON output

Techniques used to ensure determinism:
  - All timestamps come from a caller-supplied `now: Date` parameter
  - JSON.stringify with sorted keys for hash inputs
  - All sorted arrays (violations, profiles, diff results) use stable sort
    with deterministic tiebreakers (policyId ASC, etc.)
  - No Math.random(), no crypto.randomUUID(), no Date.now() inside pure functions
  - All IDs are derived deterministically from inputs (e.g., "gevpkg:<scope>-<nowMs>")

This guarantees that tests with a fixed clock are fully reproducible and that
evidence packages generated from the same data at the same timestamp are identical.

E6  Package Signing (P11-F)
    packageIntegrityHash is a SHA-256 content hash — not signed.

    as a platform secret) would make the integrity check non-repudiable.
    This is a significant security engineering task and requires key management
    infrastructure.

F)  OUT-OF-SCOPE / INTENTIONALLY DEFERRED
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

F1  External Regulator Submission
    Deliberately not implemented. No HTTP call from the governance stack to
    any external compliance system. Deferred indefinitely — requires legal
    and regulatory analysis per jurisdiction.

F2  Legal Automation
    The governance stack provides evidence. Legal interpretation and action
    based on that evidence is a human responsibility. Automating legal
    conclusions is out of scope indefinitely.

F3  AI / ML Classification
    All classification is rule-based and deterministic. ML-based classification
    (e.g., anomaly detection, predictive escalation) is intentionally excluded
    from Phase 11 due to non-determinism, explainability, and auditability
    concerns. Any future AI layer must be implemented separately and must not
    replace existing deterministic classifiers.

F4  Blockchain / Distributed Ledger
    The hash-linked chain (P11-A) is an in-process SHA-256 integrity mechanism,
    not a distributed ledger. Implementing a blockchain-based audit chain would
    require consensus infrastructure and is out of scope for an internal
    operations platform.

F5  Real-Time Policy Evaluation (event-driven P11-B)
    Policy evaluation is currently on-demand (called by route handlers).
    An event-driven P11-B that evaluates policies immediately when a new audit
    chain entry is inserted (via PostgreSQL triggers or a pub/sub channel)
    is a future architecture enhancement — it would require careful human-governed
    workflow controls to prevent the event-driven evaluation from triggering
    automatic actions.

F6  Autonomous Disciplinary Actions
    No automated suspension, deactivation, or restriction of any account,
    workspace, or user based on governance findings. This is a permanent
    out-of-scope item for the governance stack as designed. Any future
    enforcement mechanism requires a separate, human-approved enforcement
    architecture with legal review.

The platform has completed the construction of a robust, enterprise-grade
governance and compliance foundation. Platform activity is now recorded in a
tamper-detectable audit trail — every significant operational event is
preserved with a cryptographic integrity check that makes unauthorized
modification immediately visible. Built on top of this record, a policy
evaluation engine continuously detects deviations from eight governance
standards, ranging from audit completeness to execution integrity. Each
detected deviation can be assigned to an operator through a structured
investigation lifecycle, with full attribution at every stage.

The platform can now produce a clear, measurable picture of its governance
health at any point in time: how many investigations are open, how quickly
violations are being resolved, which policies are triggering most frequently,
and whether the overall governance posture meets the threshold for production
readiness. This picture is packaged into structured evidence bundles — scoped
to the platform, to a specific workspace, or to a specific entity or event —
with a content hash that allows verification of package integrity.

The entire architecture is advisory and human-governed. Every classification
is deterministic and rule-based. No enforcement is automatic. No external
party receives data without a human initiating the transfer. The foundation
is ready for the next phase of maturity: frontend governance dashboards, a
controlled export rendering layer, and workspace-scoped audit packaging.
